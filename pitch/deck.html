The problem with
permissioned RWA tokens

ERC-3643 (T-REX) is the de-facto standard for compliant tokenized assets. But its identity registry is fully public.

🚨

Full investor list is public

Anyone can call isVerified(addr) and enumerate every whitelisted holder via IdentityRegistered events. Competitor funds, journalists, and attackers get the same view as regulators.

📡

Front-running & reconnaissance

Knowing which addresses are KYC'd for a fund reveals LP allocation strategy. Social engineering targets become trivially identifiable. For family offices and institutional LPs, this is a non-starter.

⚖️

Compliance is non-negotiable

Regulated assets require on-chain transfer gating. You can't just skip KYC. The challenge is enforcing compliance without leaking the identity set.

🏛️

Institutional adoption blocked

Tokenized bonds, funds, and real estate on HashKey Chain need a privacy-preserving compliance layer. ERC-3643's exposure is the concrete obstacle to institutional RWA adoption.

Our solution: ZK-RWA Allowlist

The issuer's holder list stays off-chain as a Semaphore group. Only the Merkle root is published. Holders self-admit by ZK proof.

1

Off-chain group

Issuer adds KYC'd commitments to a Semaphore group. Full list never touches the chain.

2

Publish root

Issuer calls updateRoot(). Only the Merkle root goes on-chain.

3

Self-admission

Holder generates ZK proof in-browser (~3s) and submits admit(). No link to commitment.

4

Transfer

Standard ERC-20 transfer. Hook checks both parties are admitted. No per-tx proof overhead.
